Self-Determination Theory

How can you use Choice, Connection, and Competence - the blocks of Self-Determination Theory - to nurture motivation and creativity?

Summary

People have the “innate tendency and desire to thrive” (Fowler, 2023), but they require nurturing. How can you use Choice, Connection, and Competence - the blocks of Self-Determination Theory - to nurture motivation and creativity?

Choice / Autonomy

Choice: “Our human need to perceive we have choices, feel we have options within boundaries, and have a sense of control that we are the source of our actions” (Fowler, 2023)

People experience autonomy when they feel like they have control or a choice about the work they do (Fowler, 2023). This doesn’t mean completely hands-off leadership - just belief that people can influence their work or workplace gives a feeling of autonomy and increases productivity.

Connection / Relatedness

Connection: “Our need to feel a sense of belonging and genuine connection to others without concerns about ulterior motives, to align goals and actions to meaningful values and a sense of purpose, and to contribute to something greater than ourselves” (Fowler, 2023)

Your work day, including commute, getting ready, etc., might take up 75% of your waking hours. Have you ever had one coworker at work who makes the day fly by and brightens your atmosphere? That is authentic connection, and people thrive on it.

Competence

Competence: “Our need to feel effective at meeting everyday situations, demonstrate skill over time, and feel a sense of growth and flourishing” (Fowler, 2023)

People like learning and growing. Think of a toddler’s one question to everything: “Why?”. However, most organizations only teach people the basic hard skills they need. If you want to invest in “people’s capacity to excel” (Fowler, 2023), help them learn and grow.
